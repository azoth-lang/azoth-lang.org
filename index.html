Most languages are marred by poor decisions or foot-guns like monkey patching or arbitrary operator overloading. All the elements for a great language are now in place and common patterns are becoming apparent. We just need to learn the lessons of history and give the language enough time to gel into a cohesive whole composed of orthogonal parts before locking in bad design. — Jeff Walker, The Azoth Language Designer
